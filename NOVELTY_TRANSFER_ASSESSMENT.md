# Avatar Head Tracking: Novelty + Transfer Assessment

Date: 2026-06-14
Scope: `pipeline_memoji_rig_v15.py`, `pipeline_live_causal_v1.py`, `RESEARCH_INNOVATE_TRACKING.md`, `notes_grid_confirm.md`, `notes_live_causal.md`

## Executive Verdict

We found good engineering solutions. I do **not** see evidence that any individual technique is genuinely novel. The honest framing is:

- The components are mostly standard: Kalman covariance weighting, tracking-by-detection, landmark anchors, occlusion prediction/HOLD, head detectors, and IMM filters all have prior art.
- The specific combination is useful and somewhat uncommon in this exact avatar-head-anchor stack: MediaPipe face/pose + YOLO face + 6DRepNet360 + unified ear midpoint + source-aware Kalman + causal IMM, all without CUDA.
- The current evidence proves local improvement on one 847-frame clip. It does not prove general 360-degree head-pose correctness, multi-person robustness, real 29 fps live operation, or novelty.

Recommended public/internal wording:

> We implemented a pragmatic, uncertainty-aware face/head tracking stack that improves continuity by unifying detector anchors and weighting each measurement source by reliability. It is locally validated on one clip, Apple-Silicon-compatible, and produces an output every frame. It should be treated as an engineering integration, not a novel tracking algorithm.

Avoid:

> novel, first, solved 360 head tracking, real-time at source fps, back-of-head pose tracking, proved generalizable.

## Prior-Art Baseline

Relevant prior art and product baselines:

- MediaPipe Face Landmarker already outputs 3D landmarks, blendshapes, and transformation matrices for avatars and live/video modes: <https://developers.google.com/edge/mediapipe/solutions/vision/face_landmarker>
- MediaPipe Pose Landmarker outputs body landmarks in image/world coordinates and supports live/video modes: <https://developers.google.com/edge/mediapipe/solutions/vision/pose_landmarker>
- MediaPipe Face Detection has long exposed face keypoints including left/right ear tragion: <https://mediapipe.readthedocs.io/en/latest/solutions/face_detection.html>
- 6DRepNet360 is an open PyTorch implementation for full-range rotation head pose estimation, TIP 2024: <https://github.com/thohemp/6DRepNet360>
- OpenSeeFace is a real-time avatar-oriented face/landmark tracker and explicitly targets stable avatar animation across wide head poses: <https://github.com/emilianavt/OpenSeeFace>
- OpenFace is an open facial behavior toolkit for landmarks, head pose, action units, and gaze: <https://github.com/tadasbaltrusaitis/openface>
- SORT established a simple online tracker using a Kalman filter + Hungarian association, and notes detector quality strongly affects tracking: <https://arxiv.org/abs/1602.00763>
- ByteTrack recovers occluded/low-score objects by associating almost every detection box with tracklets: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.06864>
- ConfTrack and related MOT work adapt Kalman updates/noise to detection confidence, directly undercutting novelty claims around confidence/source-aware observation noise: <https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/WACV2024/papers/Jung_ConfTrack_Kalman_Filter-Based_Multi-Person_Tracking_by_Utilizing_Confidence_Score_of_WACV_2024_paper.pdf>
- Standard Kalman tracking docs explicitly describe the process-noise/measurement-noise tradeoff and constant-velocity tracking models: <https://www.mathworks.com/help/fusion/ug/tuning-kalman-filter-to-improve-state-estimation.html>
- IMM filters with CV/CA models are classical maneuvering-target tracking, not new: <https://www.jhuapl.edu/techdigest/content/techdigest/pdf/V22-N04/22-04-Genovese.pdf>
- SCUT-HEAD and CrowdHuman show that "head not face" detection, including crowded/occluded heads, is already a known detection problem: <https://github.com/HCIILAB/SCUT-HEAD-Dataset-Release>, <https://arxiv.org/abs/1805.00123>
- CroHD explicitly tracks pedestrian heads in dense crowds: <https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/CVPR2021/papers/Sundararaman_Tracking_Pedestrian_Heads_in_Dense_Crowd_CVPR_2021_paper.pdf>
- FLAME, video-head-tracker, and VHAP show heavier 3D head tracking/alignment approaches already exist: <https://flame.is.tue.mpg.de/>, <https://github.com/philgras/video-head-tracker>, <https://github.com/ShenhanQian/VHAP>
- PyTorch/Ultralytics support Apple Silicon MPS, so "MPS-compatible YOLO/PyTorch inference" is not novel: <https://developer.apple.com/metal/pytorch/>, <https://docs.ultralytics.com/modes/train/#apple-silicon-mps-training>
- Driver monitoring already uses facial landmarks, head posture, and eye analysis: <https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-023-44955-1>
- Head-pose estimation surveys cover full-range pose, HCI, surveillance, robotics, and related applications: <https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10462-024-10936-7>
- Human-pose estimation surveys cover motion analysis, AR/VR, HCI, healthcare, and occlusion challenges: <https://arxiv.org/html/2012.13392>

## A. Novelty Assessment

### 1. Source-aware heteroscedastic multi-detector head-anchor Kalman fusion

Local technique:

- v15 assigns per-source observation variance: `face_ear=15^2`, `pose_calib=45^2`, `pose_raw=80^2`, `hold=500^2`.
- The Kalman update weights measurements by source reliability, with confidence inflation for pose sources.

Prior art:

- Kalman filters standardly use measurement noise covariance `R` to control how much a measurement is trusted. MathWorks' tracking docs describe exactly this process-noise/measurement-noise tuning principle.
- Tracking-by-detection systems such as SORT use Kalman filters for online object tracking.
- ConfTrack and StrongSORT-style "noise scale adaptive" Kalman work already adapt Kalman behavior using detection confidence.
- Multi-sensor/track fusion has long handled different sensors/tracks with different covariances.

Verdict: **component standard; specific application/combination known-uncommon, not novel.**

What is defensible:

- "We fixed a real bug by using per-source observation uncertainty instead of treating face, pose-calib, pose-raw, and hold as equally reliable."
- "This is a standard tracking idea applied cleanly to our face/head-anchor stack."

What is not defensible:

- "Novel heteroscedastic Kalman face tracking."
- "Invented source-aware detector fusion."

Confidence: high.

### 2. Ear-midpoint pose-invariant anchor unification

Local technique:

- MediaPipe face frames use the midpoint of face mesh landmarks 234 and 454.
- Pose fallback uses MediaPipe Pose ears 7 and 8.
- Calibration maps pose anchor to the same anatomical reference, reducing v14's reference mismatch.

Prior art:

- Facial and pose systems have used anatomical landmarks for a long time.
- MediaPipe Face Detection exposes ear tragion keypoints; Face Landmarker exposes dense 3D face landmarks; Pose Landmarker exposes body landmarks.
- Avatar systems already use facial landmarks as stable control signals.

Important limitation:

- "Pose-invariant" is too strong. The ear midpoint is more consistent than nose/bbox-center across detectors, but it is not invariant under severe yaw, occlusion, hair, back-of-head, or landmark hallucination. It is an anchor convention, not a guaranteed physical skull-center measurement.

Verdict: **component standard; specific face-mesh-to-body-pose ear anchor unification is a known-uncommon application detail, not proven novel.**

What is defensible:

- "We standardized all detectors onto a single anatomical anchor, which reduced measured calibration residuals on the clip."

What is not defensible:

- "Novel pose-invariant anchor."
- "Back-view-stable anatomical head center."

Confidence: high.

### 3. Never-drop 360-degree tracking including back-of-head: face -> body-pose -> HOLD cascade

Local technique:

- Face visible: MediaPipe face anchor.
- Face absent but YOLO face/6DRepNet available: REP360 head pose plus body-pose anchor.
- Face/head-pose unavailable: mode becomes HOLD; v15 still used `pose_raw(pose_both_ears)` for the 15 HOLD frames.
- The rig emits every frame. Rotation/blendshapes may be decayed or held when the face is not visible.

Prior art:

- Prediction through occlusion and track continuity are standard in tracking-by-detection.
- ByteTrack explicitly addresses low-score/occluded detections and fragmented trajectories.
- SCUT-HEAD, CrowdHuman, and CroHD show that head detection/tracking independent of face visibility is established prior art.
- Body-pose landmarks for recovering approximate head location are standard engineering practice, even if less accurate than a real head detector.

Important limitation:

- This is **not** true 360-degree head-pose tracking. For true back-of-head, the current stack does not estimate new facial expression, does not prove yaw/roll/pitch correctness, and does not use a head-class detector. It maintains output continuity and approximate position through body-pose ears.

Verdict: **known-uncommon combination for avatar continuity; not novel; "360" is only output-continuity/position-through-pose, not proved 360 pose correctness.**

What is defensible:

- "The system never drops rendering/anchor output on this clip."
- "Back-of-head frames are position-anchored by body pose when pose ears are visible; face pose is held/decayed."

What is not defensible:

- "Solved back-of-head tracking."
- "Proved 360-degree face/head tracking."

Confidence: high for position-continuity claim on this clip; low for general back-of-head correctness.

### 4. IMM causal filter: CV + near-constant-acceleration

Local technique:

- Live v1 replaces offline RTS smoothing with causal IMM Kalman.
- Model 0: constant velocity.
- Model 1: near-constant acceleration / maneuver model.
- Per-source `R_t` is retained.

Prior art:

- IMM filters with CV/CA/turn models are classical maneuvering-target tracking.
- SORT and many MOT systems use constant-velocity Kalman models.
- MathWorks and tracking literature describe CV/CA models and IMM filtering as standard choices for targets with changing dynamics.

Important limitation:

- In `notes_live_causal.md`, NCA probability stayed low on this clip. That means the local run did not strongly prove the maneuver model's benefit. It did show the causal output stayed close to offline v15, but not that it solved a hard zoom/snap case.

Verdict: **standard component; application to this avatar stack is known-uncommon but not novel; maneuver benefit remains unconfirmed.**

What is defensible:

- "We built a causal standard IMM filter that preserves local quality vs offline v15."
- "It is a principled upgrade path for sudden motion, but we still need stress clips where NCA activates."

What is not defensible:

- "Novel live head-tracking filter."
- "Proved rapid-zoom robustness."

Confidence: high for non-novel classification; medium for practical benefit until stress-tested.

### 5. Whole open-weight, Apple-Silicon/MPS-only, real-time, never-drop stack

Local technique:

- Uses MediaPipe Face/Pose, YOLOv10n-face, 6DRepNet360, NumPy/SciPy filtering, MPS where PyTorch models run.
- Achieved 24.9 fps on M3 Ultra, below the 29 fps source rate.
- Emits an anchor every frame on the test clip.

Prior art:

- MediaPipe and OpenSeeFace already support real-time face/landmark/avatar use cases.
- OpenFace provides open-source facial landmark/head-pose/action-unit/gaze tooling.
- PyTorch MPS and Ultralytics MPS support are documented.
- 6DRepNet360 is open PyTorch code/weights for full-range head pose.

Important wording correction:

- "MPS-only" is inaccurate. MediaPipe Tasks are CPU/XNNPACK in this setup, and NumPy filtering is CPU. The accurate phrase is **CUDA-free / Apple-Silicon-compatible**, with PyTorch models using MPS.
- "Real-time" is conditional: 24.9 fps on M3 Ultra is acceptable for 24 fps, but not proven at 29 fps source-rate live streaming.
- "Open-weight" should be license-audited before external claims. Some components are open-source or provide downloadable weights, but that is not the same as unrestricted commercial/open-weight licensing for the full stack.
- "Never-drop" means output continuity, not guaranteed measurement validity.

Verdict: **known-uncommon product integration; not a novel algorithm; only locally validated.**

What is defensible:

- "A CUDA-free Apple-Silicon-compatible prototype that outputs every frame and locally matches offline v15 within 50 px on the test clip."

What is not defensible:

- "First open-weight MPS-only real-time never-drop 360 tracker."
- "Production-real-time at 29 fps."
- "Fully open-weight/commercially clean" without a license audit.

Confidence: high.

## B. Transfer Assessment

### 1. VTubing, telepresence, and avatar puppeteering

Mapping:

- Ear-midpoint unification stabilizes avatar placement when face landmarks, face detector boxes, and body pose disagree.
- Source-aware `R_t` prevents low-confidence fallback anchors from pulling high-confidence face frames.
- Never-drop output continuity avoids avatar disappearance.

Already done:

- MediaPipe Face Landmarker explicitly targets avatars.
- OpenSeeFace is already optimized for avatar-useful landmarks and stable wide-pose tracking.
- OpenFace covers landmarks/head pose/action units/gaze.

Added value:

- Moderate. The value is in the fallback engineering and consistent anchor convention, especially for offline clips or single-user streams with large head turns.
- Not a replacement for OpenSeeFace/ARKit-class facial tracking; use as a continuity layer or post-processing stack.

### 2. Driver monitoring and attention estimation

Mapping:

- Head pose and eye/face landmarks are used to infer attention/drowsiness.
- Source-aware fusion could combine face landmarks, head detector, cabin/body pose, IR confidence, and temporary HOLD during occlusion.
- IMM can smooth rapid head turns without excessive lag.

Already done:

- Driver monitoring systems already use facial landmark estimation, head posture, and eye-area analysis; see Kim et al. 2023 Scientific Reports.
- Head-pose estimation surveys list driver monitoring, surveillance, robotics, HCI, and full-range pose as established application areas.

Added value:

- Potentially useful for continuity during brief face occlusion, hand-over-face, profile turn, sunglasses/lighting issues.
- Must not be used for safety decisions without independent validation, calibrated uncertainty, and failure-state reporting. "Never-drop" is dangerous wording in safety contexts because it can hide low-confidence inference.

### 3. Human-robot interaction, social presence, and meeting analytics

Mapping:

- Anchor continuity and yaw/pitch estimates can drive who-is-looking-where, turn-taking, engagement, or robot attention.
- Body-pose fallback helps when the face is not visible but head/shoulders remain visible.

Already done:

- Head-pose estimation is a standard signal in HCI/robotics.
- OpenFace/OpenFace 3.0 style toolkits already provide facial behavior signals for interaction research.

Added value:

- Good as a robust single-person tracker when preserving temporal continuity matters.
- Weak for multi-person scenes unless identity association is added. Current pipeline uses `num_faces=1` and highest-confidence YOLO face; it is not identity-safe.

### 4. Crowd, surveillance, and sports broadcast head tracking

Mapping:

- Replace face detector fallback with a head detector; keep Kalman/IMM and per-source `R`.
- Ear-midpoint anchor becomes less useful at distance; head-box center or learned head center may be better.

Already done:

- SCUT-HEAD, CrowdHuman, and CroHD directly cover head detection/tracking in crowds.
- ByteTrack/SORT style MOT already solves detector-to-track association.

Added value:

- Low-to-moderate. Our stack is not competitive as a crowd tracker without multi-object data association, ReID, and head-class detection.
- The per-source uncertainty pattern transfers well, but the rest should be rebuilt around standard MOT.

### 5. Sports biomechanics, rehab, posture coaching, and movement analysis

Mapping:

- Pose fallback and IMM smoothing can maintain head position during body movement, camera motion, or temporary face loss.
- Source-aware fusion can combine body keypoints, face landmarks, and manual/IMU signals.

Already done:

- Human pose estimation is widely used for motion analysis, AR/VR, HCI, healthcare, and sports/training.
- MediaPipe Pose already outputs landmarks suitable for posture/movement analysis.

Added value:

- Useful for single-person consumer video where the head is part of a larger body-motion track.
- For clinical/rehab use, it needs ground-truth validation and confidence reporting; current one-clip avatar validation is insufficient.

### 6. Video post-production, AR filters, and privacy-preserving local effects

Mapping:

- Never-drop output continuity prevents filters/avatars from popping off.
- Ear-anchor consistency reduces mode-switch jumps.
- Apple-Silicon-compatible processing supports local/private workflows without a cloud dependency.

Already done:

- MediaPipe, ARKit-like systems, OpenSeeFace, and FLAME-based offline trackers all exist.
- FLAME/video-head-tracker/VHAP are stronger when high-quality offline head geometry matters, but heavier and often not real-time or not Mac-friendly.

Added value:

- Good for pragmatic, local batch processing where "good enough every frame" beats perfect offline reconstruction.
- Not state of the art for high-fidelity 3D head alignment.

## What Should Be Tested Next

Minimum tests before stronger claims:

1. Multi-clip benchmark: at least 20 clips spanning frontal, profile, back-of-head, fast zoom, low light, glasses, hair occlusion, and multi-person distractors.
2. Independent annotation: manually label head center on sampled frames; do not use v15 as ground truth for v15/live claims.
3. Ablations:
   - v14 vs v15 ear anchor only.
   - constant `R` vs heteroscedastic `R`.
   - CV-only causal vs IMM causal.
   - face detector only vs face+pose vs face+pose+head detector.
4. True live test: camera loop at 29/30 fps with frame-drop accounting, end-to-end latency, and weaker Apple hardware.
5. Head-detector test: add SCUT/CrowdHuman-style head detector for back-of-head; separately evaluate position recovery and pose correctness.
6. Multi-person false-positive test: introduce another face/body and measure identity switches.

## Bottom Line

We did find useful engineering solutions:

- Unified anatomical anchor across detectors.
- Source-aware measurement uncertainty in the Kalman filter.
- Causal IMM version that preserves offline quality on the current clip.
- A CUDA-free Apple-Silicon-compatible stack with every-frame output.

But the honest novelty answer is:

- These are not new algorithms.
- The combination is practical and somewhat uncommon for this exact avatar-head-tracking setup.
- The back-of-head / 360 claim should be downgraded to "output continuity with body-pose position fallback; orientation/expression held when the face is unavailable."
- The live claim should be "24 fps-class on M3 Ultra, not yet proven at 29/30 fps source-rate live."

The techniques transfer well to other continuity-sensitive tracking systems, especially avatar/AR/video post workflows. They add less value in domains that already use mature MOT/head detectors unless we add identity association, head-class detection, and independent uncertainty calibration.
